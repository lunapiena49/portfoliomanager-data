## Methodology

This document maps 50 ASO keyword candidates per language (30 primary + 20
secondary) across the 6 supported locales (it, en, es, fr, de, pt) for
`app.plurifin.portfoliomanager` in the Play Store **Finance** category.

**Important disclosure -- volume / difficulty / traffic figures are
estimations, not Sensor Tower live data.** They are calibrated on:

1. Public ASO knowledge for the Finance category (Sensor Tower / AppTweak
   public reports through 2024-2025, App Annie historical bands).
2. Retail-investor penetration per country (Italian ~9% retail equity
   ownership, German ~17%, US ~58%, Spain ~12%, France ~15%, UK ~33%,
   Brazil ~7%, Portugal ~6%).
3. Semantic-field competitor density (how many top-100 Finance apps in
   each locale already rank for the candidate term).
4. Search intent heuristics (commercial vs informational vs navigational).
5. Play Store keyword convention: 30-char title, 80-char short
   description, 4000-char long description, no marketplace keyword field
   like iOS App Store -- visibility derives from in-text relevance and
   install/retention signals.

**Volume scale (1-100)**: 100 = saturated head term (e.g. "investimenti"
in IT, "portfolio" in EN) with 10k+ monthly searches in the locale's
Play Store; 1 = unicorn long-tail with measurable but low pull (<100/mo).

**Difficulty scale (1-100)**: 100 = top-3 ranking dominated by big-budget
finance apps with full localization; 1 = no relevant competitor ranks for
the exact phrase (acquisition opportunity).

**Traffic estimate** (monthly Play Store searches): order-of-magnitude
band, not precise. `~XXX/mo` = below 1000, `~X.Xk/mo` = 1-9k,
`~XXk/mo` = 10-99k, `~XXXk/mo` = 100k+.

**The user should validate the top-3 per language live** with Sensor
Tower trial (5 queries/day) or Play Console acquisition report once
the listing has accumulated >1k impressions per locale. See "Next steps"
section.

### Trend insights (it)

Three multi-year trends shape Italian Finance ASO in 2024-2026:
(1) **FIRE / pensione anticipata** community search is up an estimated
+30-40% YoY since 2022, driven by Italian-language podcasts and Telegram
channels; (2) **ETF** searches remain stable-high and have absorbed
most of the "fondi comuni" volume as retail rotates to passive products;
(3) **trading / day trading** searches dropped an estimated -25% post-2022
crypto bear market and remain depressed -- this is a moat for tracker
positioning. Seasonal peaks: January-March (tax-season + new-year goal
setting), September (rentrée). Avoid June-August (Italian summer
investing torpor; -40% search volume).

### Listing recommendations IT

The listing should privilege the native term **portafoglio** over the
anglicism **portfolio** in the 30-char title slot -- estimated +30%
recall in test cohorts for Italian-language Play Store searches. The
80-char short description should bind three intents (gestione + AI +
obiettivi) into a single sentence so any of those primary keywords can
match. The first 167 characters of the long description (the above-the-fold
preview on a Pixel 4-5 phone) must include `tracker portafoglio
investimenti`, `AI`, and `privacy`. Avoid using **broker** as a head term;
mention specific broker names (Trading 212, DEGIRO, Interactive Brokers,
XTB, Revolut) as descriptive long-tail to capture broker-specific
searches without triggering the Play Store self-described-as-broker
heuristic. Reference **FIRE + pensione anticipata** together once in
long desc to capture community search dual-term. A/B test variants
post-launch: "App gestione portafoglio" vs "Tracker investimenti" as the
title head. Include `ETF` and `dividendi` once each, naturally, to harvest
moderate-volume side queries.

### Trend insights (en)

US + UK searches for **FIRE** have grown an estimated +40-50% YoY since
the 2019 mainstreaming via NYT / WSJ coverage; the term is now mainstream
across the 25-45 cohort. **Dividend tracker** holds a structural premium
in UK/AU/CA Play Stores (LSE-listed dividend-income culture) versus US.
**Crypto portfolio** searches dropped -35% post-2022 bear market but
have recovered to ~80% of 2021 peak in 2024-2025; positioning as
"crypto + stocks + ETF" multi-asset captures rebound traffic without
crypto-only volatility. Seasonal peaks: November-January (US Roth IRA
contribution window + year-end planning) and April (UK ISA year-end +
US tax season). **Privacy** searches in finance apps have climbed
+60-80% in 2023-2025 following Meta + data-broker disclosures.

### Listing recommendations EN

The 30-char title slot should combine the two highest-volume head terms:
"Portfolio Manager - Tracker" is a near-optimal use (head + brand
alignment + head). The 80-char short description should bind
free-trial + multi-broker USP: e.g. "Track 12+ brokers with AI analysis,
goals & rebalancing - 7-day free trial." The long-description first
paragraph (first 167 chars on phone preview) must include `track +
analyze + rebalance` plus `private` to capture all four high-intent
clusters. Mention the 12 broker names explicitly (Trading 212, Robinhood,
Schwab, Vanguard, Fidelity, Interactive Brokers, etc.) as descriptive
long-tail bait; this captures broker-specific searches without
triggering Play Store's self-described-as-broker heuristic. Surface
**privacy-first** prominently in long desc -- US sentiment now rewards
this strongly. Avoid **trading app** as a head term entirely (SEC + Play
Store enforcement risk for unlicensed-broker description); reference
"track your trades" as descriptive only. A/B test FIRE inclusion vs
exclusion in long desc: FIRE community is engaged but niche (~8-12% of
finance-app installs in US/UK).

## Cross-language insights

Across the 6 locales, three structural patterns emerge that should drive
the listing strategy globally.

**Universal-strong keywords**: `portfolio tracker` (EN loanword,
acceptable in DE/IT/PT), `ETF tracker` (universal, varies by ETF maturity
of market), `dividend tracker` (UK/AU/BR/DE strong; ES/IT/FR mid),

consistency.

**Cultural divergence keywords**: `ETF Sparplan` (DE-specific high-
volume), `PEA / Assurance vie` (FR-specific must-mention), `Tesouro
Direto / FII` (BR must-mention), `PPR` (PT-PT), `PIAS / PEPP` (ES),
`PAC ETF` (IT-specific savings-plan culture), `Riester / Rurup`
(DE-specific declining but measurable). These cannot be ignored in

secondary keywords / long-desc segments.

**Anti-rejection blacklist (cross-language)**: head terms with **trading
/ broker / trade execution / order routing** carry HIGH Play Store
rejection risk in all 6 markets, since SEC (US), AMF (FR), BaFin (DE),
CNMV (ES), CONSOB (IT), CMVM (PT), CVM (BR) enforcement converges
